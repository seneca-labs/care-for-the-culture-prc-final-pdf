Feed Screen (Phone)

frame-feed-home
Part Number: HIV Prevention_Freddie Experiential Care for the Culture Kiosk_June 2026 US-UNBC-4189

Purpose & Intent

Summary: Public feed view available on a guest's phone after opting in. Displays photos taken tonight with the subject's first name and vibe tags. Acts as a social discovery surface only — it carries no health-related content.

User Action: Scrolls the feed, taps a photo to open a profile view, or taps filter to narrow the feed by vibe. Tapping the map toggle in the top-right switches to the Home Screen Map Toggle view.

Legal & Compliance Language

Why this language is here: The public feed intentionally contains no PrEP, HIV, health, risk, or clinical language. Only first name and social vibe tags are visible. The user's PrEP answer collected earlier in the kiosk flow is never surfaced in the feed, ensuring no patient-status disclosure to other guests.

Concerns this addresses:

  • State HIV confidentiality statutes — no linkage between feed presence and health-related response
  • Stigma risk — vibe tags are social, not status-based
  • Consent scope — only guests who opted in to the feed appear here

Legal basis: State HIV confidentiality statutes (jurisdiction-dependent). TCPA not applicable on this screen. No clinical claims surface.

Feed Screen Profile View (Phone)

frame-feed-profile
Part Number: HIV Prevention_Freddie Experiential Care for the Culture Kiosk_June 2026 US-UNBC-4189

Purpose & Intent

Summary: Expanded profile after tapping a feed photo. Displays the guest's vibe tags (social mood preferences selected earlier on the Vibe Check screen) and a single "send a compliment" action. No bio, no health info, no free-text field.

User Action: Views vibe tags and chooses to send a pre-written compliment, or dismisses the view to return to the feed.

Legal & Compliance Language

Why this language is here: Vibe tags are social descriptors — they describe mood and social intent ("here to dance", "game to flirt", "ready to turn up"), not health behaviors or risk profiles. The profile view deliberately surfaces zero data that could be cross-referenced with the user's PrEP answer or zip code from the kiosk onboarding.

Concerns this addresses:

  • No linkage between social profile and user's PrEP answer / HIV data / county statistics
  • "send a compliment" is a constrained positive interaction (see next slide) — no DMs, no free text
  • De-identification: first name only, no last name, no phone, no address

Legal basis: State HIV confidentiality statutes. Safety-by-design: constrained interaction model reduces harassment and outing risk.

Feed Screen Compliment View (Phone)

frame-feed-compliment
Part Number: HIV Prevention_Freddie Experiential Care for the Culture Kiosk_June 2026 US-UNBC-4189

Purpose & Intent

Summary: Modal sheet for sending a compliment to another guest. The user selects one of four pre-written positive statements. There is no free-text input. The recipient sees the compliment as a notification; the sender is not revealed unless both parties have mutually exchanged contact details through the app.

User Action: Taps one of the pre-written compliments to send, or taps "cancel" to dismiss.

Delivery Mechanism: When a compliment is sent, it is delivered as an SMS text to the recipient from the feed's shared phone number — not from the sender's personal number. The sender's identity is not revealed. This keeps the interaction anonymous and makes the party feel more welcoming by letting guests spread warmth without social risk.

Legal & Compliance Language

Why this language is here: Zero free-text input is a deliberate safety decision. All four compliments are affirming and focused on photo, outfit, energy, or presence — never on body, health, status, or sexual attributes. This structurally prevents harassment, unsolicited sexual messages, and accidental or intentional outing.

Anonymous SMS delivery: Compliments are sent from the feed's single shared phone number. The recipient sees a text from the feed — never the sender's personal number. This removes outing risk, prevents any phone-number disclosure between guests, and reinforces the curated, welcoming tone of the experience.

Concerns this addresses:

  • Harassment and unsolicited contact on a social app serving the HIV-affected community
  • Outing risk — no free text means no PrEP/HIV references can be sent
  • Phone-number privacy — senders' personal numbers are never exposed to recipients
  • Platform liability — constrained, from-the-feed-only delivery is auditable

Legal basis: State HIV confidentiality statutes; platform safety-by-design. Section 230 does not apply to first-party content; these compliments are author-approved brand content.

PrEP Disclaimer

frame-prep-disclaimer
Part Number: HIV Prevention_Freddie Experiential Care for the Culture Kiosk_June 2026 US-UNBC-4189

Purpose & Intent

Summary: Pre-experience disclaimer and PrEP primer. Discloses what data will be collected (zip, awareness of PrEP, phone number, name, Instagram) and why. Provides a baseline PrEP definition, efficacy statement, and STI disclaimer so users have the minimum medical context required to proceed. Acts as the user's opt-in consent point before any data entry.

User Action: Reads the disclaimer and taps "got it" to proceed, or declines by closing the screen.

Legal & Compliance Language

Why this language is here: This screen is the only place in the experience where full ISI-style PrEP language appears. It uses the FDA-aligned PrEP description ("pre-exposure prophylaxis"), a regulatorily-framed efficacy statement ("about 99% effective in preventing HIV through sex" — matched to Gilead-approved brand language), the required STI non-protection disclaimer, and a redirect to a healthcare provider for medical questions. This keeps downstream screens non-clinical: because the disclaimer has already been shown, later screens can use awareness/access language rather than clinical claims.

Key phrasing decisions:

  • "awareness of PrEP" — not "PrEP status" — avoids patient self-identification risk before the PrEP Question screen
  • "about 99% effective in preventing HIV through sex" — regulatorily approved efficacy statement; "about" and "through sex" are load-bearing qualifiers
  • "PrEP doesn't protect against other STIs" — required safety language
  • "talk to your healthcare provider" — redirects medical questions off-platform, preserves non-clinical positioning of the rest of the experience
  • "if you don't want to share or join the feed, you don't have to" — explicit opt-out language preserves user autonomy

Concerns this addresses:

  • FDA PrEP efficacy claim requirements
  • Required STI non-protection disclosure
  • Informed consent for data collection (zip, phone, name, Instagram)
  • Non-medical positioning of the downstream community experience

Legal basis: FDA ISI requirements for PrEP-adjacent communications; Gilead brand compliance language; state HIV confidentiality statutes; TCPA (phone number collection requires prior express consent, satisfied by the explicit opt-in acknowledged with "got it").

Summary: Color legend shown before the national and city data frames begin. Establishes the three-color visual language (on PrEP / living with HIV / neither) and frames the upcoming visualizations as awareness data rather than clinical diagnosis or surveillance.

Why this language is here: Opens with "we're about to show you how aware your community actually is" — "aware" is the PRC-approved framing from the language guidelines, in place of "protected", "at risk", or "unaware of their status". The three color labels are deliberately neutral: "on PrEP" is observational, "living with HIV" is person-first language preferred by community advocates, and "neither" is non-clinical (rather than "untreated" or "unprotected", which would make clinical claims).

Key phrasing decisions:

  • "how aware your community actually is" — aware/unaware framing per guidelines
  • "prevalence data" — observational, not causal
  • "living with HIV" — person-first, community-preferred
  • "neither" — neutral third category, no clinical inference

Concerns this addresses:

  • No clinical claims in the color key or subsequent frames
  • No fear-based or stigmatizing framing
  • Consistent visual grammar applied across every data frame that follows

Legal basis: Language guidelines approved for PRC review; no clinical claims. Public data context applies (AIDSVu 2023 is cited on each subsequent data frame).
